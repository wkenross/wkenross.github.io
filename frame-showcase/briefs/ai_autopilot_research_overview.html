AI Autopilot Research Overview

FRAME is exploring near-autopilot workflow support for application management, lead generation, RFP response, and sales follow-up. The goal is not unattended submissions. The goal is a safer reviewed lane where AI can prepare decisions, draft artifacts, operate known browser flows, route exceptions, and produce audit notes while a person remains accountable for final action.

Capability Ladder

Level Capability FRAME Use
Current registries CSV/JSON recommendation lists and wizards already useful, inspectable, and low-risk
Durable memory prior decisions, skip/apply reasons, wizard outcomes supports better recommendations over time
Structured AI reviewer returns action, confidence, risks, and next wizard useful only with regression tests and review gates
Browser helper operates known pages in dry-run or assisted mode limited to allow-listed workflows
Reviewed autopilot handles low-risk cases and parks exceptions requires audit logs and explicit stop conditions

What The Research Evaluates

  • AI model calls and structured outputs
  • memory and retrieval over prior decisions
  • browser automation options and dry-run evidence
  • local workstation requirements
  • cloud browser options when local sessions are brittle
  • safety gates for sensitive or account-changing actions
  • Agile spike framing before implementation stories

Review Gates

The framework should stop or park work when it sees identity uncertainty, MFA, payment, legal/compliance answers, private data, unexpected browser state, or any final submit/send/publish/account-changing action without explicit review.

This keeps AI positioned as a practical workflow accelerator, not a black-box decision maker.
