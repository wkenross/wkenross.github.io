AI Tooling Review-First Pattern

FRAME uses generative AI as a practical workbench layer for product, sales, marketing, BI, and agile workflows.

The pattern is review-first: AI helps collect and structure inputs, compare requirements against evidence, draft artifacts, score options, and prepare next-step workflows. A person remains responsible for final submissions, outreach, publication, account changes, and business commitments.

Where AI Fits

Area FRAME Use
Requirements Summarize long notes, extract acceptance criteria, and shape backlog-ready stories.
Sales and marketing Draft reviewed outreach, recommendation lists, RFP responses, and follow-up packets.
BI and data Inspect data quality, explain reports, prepare SQL/Power BI handoff notes, and document anomalies.
Agile delivery Maintain planning notes, decision logs, release plans, and technical project management evidence.

Control Points

  • source files remain visible and inspectable
  • generated outputs are treated as drafts until reviewed
  • scripts capture manifests and run notes where possible
  • browser, email, publication, and account-changing actions require explicit

    • operator checkpoints

This makes AI a source of speed and structure without letting it become an unreviewed black box.
