Product Discovery Tooling

The adapter work is not only an integration exercise. It also needs product discovery in partnership with engineering, CX, commercial teams, procurement stakeholders, and customers. FRAME can jump-start that work with reviewed, public-safe tools:

Tool Output B2B Adapter Use
Competitive analysis Compare commerce, procurement, catalog, pricing, cart, and order-management patterns across providers and peers.
Product SWOT Separate strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats before turning research into roadmap candidates.
Partner matrix Compare JAGGAER, Coupa, SAP Ariba, Oracle, Workday, and other ecosystem partners by capability, integration pattern, maturity, risk, and discovery questions.
Market research brief Synthesize public evidence, CX signals, product behavior, and integration trends into actionable insight.
Customer interview brief Create an interview.md-style guide for buyer, procurement, IT, commercial, CX, and support conversations.

This is the link between research and product delivery: market behavior and CX signals become prioritized roadmap choices, dashboard metrics, epics, stories, acceptance criteria, and sprint work.

Research-To-Dashboard Flow

MilliporeSigma's public eCommerce Solutions page describes e-Procurement as system-to-system connectivity with existing spend-management systems and names providers such as JAGGAER, Ariba, SAP, Oracle, and Coupa. It also describes a three-step implementation pattern: create a project plan, perform setup and testing, then launch with go-live monitoring.

FRAME supports that pattern with a public-safe dashboard flow:


vendor and customer research
  -> competitive analysis / SWOT / partner matrix
  -> customer interview brief
  -> product brief and roadmap
  -> provider lane and implementation boundary
  -> synthetic fixtures
  -> PowerShell validation and Python normalization
  -> SQL/BI service-health data
  -> Agile epics, user stories, and sprint evidence
  -> reviewed AI summaries and next actions

The website is the product dashboard: it shows goals, artifacts, roadmap, research, sprint scope, and claim boundary.

The BI layer is the operating dashboard: it should show event counts, exceptions, readiness, cycle time, onboarding progress, and business impact once the prototype has enough synthetic or production-approved data.

Commerce Lifecycle

The adapter dashboard tracks the full transaction lifecycle:

  1. Customer or buyer context
  2. Catalog and product identity
  3. Contract pricing, eligibility, and promotions
  4. Cart, quote, requisition, or PunchOut cart return
  5. Purchase order or order submission
  6. Order acknowledgement and fulfillment status
  7. Safety, quality, and product-document access
  8. Invoice, ASN, and reconciliation
  9. Exceptions, support, reporting, and roadmap feedback

In science-heavy, regulated, or documentation-heavy commerce, product documentation can be part of the core product experience. A useful B2B adaptor should be able to account for SDS, certificates, specification sheets, labels, and other safety or quality evidence as part of the product and order context.

Claim Boundary

FRAME does not replace procurement suites, ERP systems, CRM platforms, BI tools, Agile tools, or commerce platforms. It helps product teams see the workflow clearly enough to make those systems work together:


buyer platform
  -> adaptor
  -> B2B core capability
  -> fulfillment / finance / support
  -> reporting / roadmap learning

The public proof lane uses synthetic data and public-safe documentation. The private opportunity-specific evidence stays inside the interview and service prep folders.
