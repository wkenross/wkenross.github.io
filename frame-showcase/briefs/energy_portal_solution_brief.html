Energy Portal Solution Brief

The Energy Portal case study describes a B2B operations and commercial portal surface that connects dashboard review, contract context, counterparty and carrier workflows, transaction and invoice traceability, executive reporting, and revenue-model inputs.

The public story is not the private portal itself. The public story is how FRAME turns a complex portal environment into reviewed, repeatable automation:

  • map the product surface and page flow
  • identify source data and lineage
  • separate private/raw material from public-safe evidence
  • use scripts to validate workbook, dashboard, and reporting outputs
  • turn operational workflows into publishable product-management artifacts

What It Demonstrates

Area Public-safe story
Dashboard Business users need a central status view with drill-down paths into contracts, schedules, exceptions, and reporting.
Contracts Contract records drive customer, counterparty, date, volume, and revenue-performance workflows.
Counterparties Customer and account context connect portal operations to CRM-style review and management reporting.
Carriers Transportation partner workflows connect schedules, allocations, incidents, and operational follow-up.
Transactions Movement and quantity records become structured review inputs for reporting and exception handling.
Invoices Billing and reconciliation surfaces support cash, revenue, and exception visibility.
Executive reporting Portal activity feeds leadership-ready reporting and recurring operating review.
Revenue modeling Contract, volume, price, and forecast assumptions become monthly-series and scenario inputs.

FRAME Contribution

FRAME adds the operating layer around the portal:

  • PowerShell orchestration for repeatable run sequences
  • Python scripts for workbook validation, browser-assisted review, and report

    • generation

  • Excel-based review workbooks for operator inspection
  • SQL-ready data-model planning for durable reporting
  • BI and dashboard handoff for Power BI, Tableau, or Excel outputs
  • obfuscation rules and publication gates for public-safe examples
