Competitive Analysis And Market Intelligence Wizards

FRAME turns scattered opportunity, market, partner, and customer signals into ranked, reviewable next steps.

The same pattern that supports internal workflow automation can support product discovery, competitive analysis, SWOT planning, partner matrices, market research, customer-interview preparation, selective RFP discovery, lead-list generation, and sales follow-up:

  1. Collect public-safe signals from RFP pages, directories, imported CSVs,

    2. marketplace project listings, partner pages, product sites, analyst notes, and reviewed inbox alerts.

  2. Normalize each signal into a structured research, partner, customer, or

    3. opportunity card.

  3. Score the card for fit, value, clarity, urgency, evidence quality, and risk.
  4. Produce a recommendation list, SWOT seed, partner matrix, or customer

    5. interview brief that an operator can sort and review.

  5. Open the right source page, evidence, draft, CRM checklist, interview.md, or

    6. follow-up note through a wizard.

  6. Record the operator decision so future lists become smarter.

What Makes It Useful

The workflow is designed for busy product managers and operators who need fewer tabs and better decisions:

  • competitive signals become structured and explainable
  • product SWOT inputs are grouped by strength, weakness, opportunity, and threat
  • partner matrices compare fit, integration pattern, maturity, and risk
  • customer-interview briefs turn market research into questions to ask CX,

    • engineering, procurement, partners, and buyers

  • lead lists become structured and explainable
  • RFPs are ranked by fit and deadline
  • follow-up work is grouped by next action
  • inbox notices are routed into opportunity folders
  • CRM handoffs can be prepared from reviewed data
  • low-value or already-declined items are suppressed

Review-First Automation

The framework can prepare a next step, but important actions remain reviewed:

  • send
  • submit
  • publish
  • purchase
  • account changes
  • CRM import
  • proposal delivery

This makes the system useful for real business operations without pretending that every external action should be autonomous.

Public-Safe Positioning

This module demonstrates recommendation and workflow architecture. It does not publish private inbox contents, credentials, live selectors, or unattended response automation.

Product Risk Analysis

How FRAME Reads Procurement Risk

FRAME treats procurement risk as a product discovery funnel, not just a technical integration checklist.

Sources
Public vendor documentation, supplier enablement terms, procurement learning material, cXML and PunchOut references, Sigma-Aldrich eProcurement content, and synthetic FRAME fixtures.
Research Approach
Each provider lane is mapped by access model, setup path, transaction lifecycle, likely owner, open question, and claim boundary. No private tenant access is implied.
Risk Reporting
Risks are reported as drop-off stages: access, supplier master, catalog discovery, PunchOut auth, cart return, approval, PO, invoice/ASN, support, and adoption.
Dashboard Integration
The risk map feeds partner matrices, SWOT, sprint backlog items, launch-health metrics, and BI-ready dashboard signals such as cart return exceptions and PO-to-invoice lag.
