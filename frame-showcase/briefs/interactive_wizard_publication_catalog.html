Interactive Product Wizard Catalog

FRAME interactive wizards help an operator review recommendations and prepare next steps without losing context.

The public catalog should describe patterns, not private automation details.

Wizard Patterns

Pattern Public description
Recommendation list wizard Reviews scored rows, opens source evidence, and records operator decisions.
Application packet wizard Prepares resume paths, cover-letter drafts, and checklist notes for manual review.
Lead generator wizard Turns public-safe source cards into ranked lead reports and CRM-ready fields.
Inbox follow-up wizard Sorts opportunity, rejection, follow-up, and next-step messages after review.
Mobile requirements wizard Promotes iPhone notes into Windows workflow requirements.

Public-Safe Sample Fields

Recommendation list examples can include:

  • source
  • organization
  • opportunity title
  • public URL
  • category
  • fit score
  • speed score
  • risk score
  • recommended next action
  • operator decision
  • notes

What Not To Publish

  • Live browser profile paths.
  • Live form selectors.
  • Email account names beyond public contact information.
  • Private email subjects or bodies.
  • Platform-specific automation that could submit, send, scrape, or alter an external account without review.
  • Credentials, tokens, cookies, MFA details, or recovery information.

Public Message

These wizards demonstrate a disciplined operator workflow: gather evidence, rank the options, prepare the next step, and keep the human decision visible.

Product Risk Analysis

How FRAME Reads Procurement Risk

FRAME treats procurement risk as a product discovery funnel, not just a technical integration checklist.

Sources
Public vendor documentation, supplier enablement terms, procurement learning material, cXML and PunchOut references, Sigma-Aldrich eProcurement content, and synthetic FRAME fixtures.
Research Approach
Each provider lane is mapped by access model, setup path, transaction lifecycle, likely owner, open question, and claim boundary. No private tenant access is implied.
Risk Reporting
Risks are reported as drop-off stages: access, supplier master, catalog discovery, PunchOut auth, cart return, approval, PO, invoice/ASN, support, and adoption.
Dashboard Integration
The risk map feeds partner matrices, SWOT, sprint backlog items, launch-health metrics, and BI-ready dashboard signals such as cart return exceptions and PO-to-invoice lag.
