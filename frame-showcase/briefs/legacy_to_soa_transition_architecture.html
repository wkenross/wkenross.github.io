Legacy-To-SOA Transition Architecture

FRAME is an automation framework for service-oriented architecture and generative-AI enablement.

The practical mission is to help organizations move from closed, static, or monolithic operating models into service-oriented, cloud-queryable, BI-ready, AI-ready workflows without losing the familiar reports, controls, and business meaning that executives and operators still expect.

Automated reporting is the bridge, not a side effect. FRAME keeps stakeholder reports, dashboards, exception lists, and operating views available while the source systems are being decomposed into services, endpoints, SQL models, BI semantic layers, and AI-readable handoffs.

Core Transition Pattern


monolithic report
  -> FRAME orchestration keeps the output alive
  -> documented business meaning and reconciliation notes
  -> service boundary, endpoint, or data-product candidate
  -> SQL / BI / cloud-queryable proof
  -> AI-assisted explanation, testing, and improvement

This is not a vendor replacement claim. SAP, Oracle, Salesforce, FAME/FEDM, CRM platforms, procurement suites, and portal systems can all be useful participants. FRAME focuses on the transition artifacts that make business processes legible outside closed screens and static exports.

What FRAME Produces

  • executive-familiar reports
  • endpoint inventories
  • service-boundary notes
  • workflow maps
  • SQL checks and BI validation queries
  • procurement, CRM, finance, and reporting handoffs
  • exception reports
  • Markdown/source-controlled documentation
  • Codex-ready implementation packages
  • AI-readable context and test notes

Service Collation Model

FRAME services should be organized by business capability rather than by old project labels:

Service Area Purpose
Reports Preserve expected executive and operational outputs while source systems change; this is the first transition bridge.
Web / Portal Map user-facing screens and portal flows into service and endpoint candidates; publish the evidence without becoming the whole strategy.
Data / SQL Make source meaning queryable and testable through SQL, cloud data, or BI models.
Procurement Replicate procurement business meaning: supplier, requisition, approval, PO, receipt, invoice, exception, audit.
CRM / Revenue Keep customer, opportunity, contract, forecast, and follow-up workflows portable.
Endpoint Assurance Document, test, reconcile, observe, explain, and improve service contracts.

Guardrail

The service structure should not disrupt working orchestration. Existing nightly, intraday, approval, projection-validation, and publication scripts can continue to run where they work while FRAME documents the service boundaries they imply. Move code only after the replacement path is tested.

Why AI Needs This

Generative AI becomes useful when operations are legible: trusted data, callable endpoints, documented workflows, clear service boundaries, observable behavior, and governed actions. Stronger AI does not remove the need for service architecture; it makes missing service architecture more obvious.

Public-Safe Positioning

FRAME should describe architecture patterns, not accuse vendors. A platform becomes a monolith-risk blocker when business logic, data, workflow state, metadata, and actions are trapped behind boundaries that are costly or hard to inspect, query, automate, test, or improve.
