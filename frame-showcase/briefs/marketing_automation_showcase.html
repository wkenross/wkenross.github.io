FRAME Product Management And Marketing Discovery

FRAME Product Management and Marketing Discovery turns scattered public opportunities, market signals, partner evidence, customer notes, and inbox signals into reviewed recommendation lists and guided next steps.

The showcase pattern is useful for teams that need to monitor product and market opportunities, build qualified research lists, compare partners, prepare customer discovery, and keep follow-up work from disappearing in a crowded inbox.

What This Demonstrates

  • Themed discovery across public websites, marketplaces, and imported lead

    • files.

  • Research and opportunity cards normalized into organization, title, URL,

    • industry, scope, urgency, evidence, and next-step fields.

  • Recommendation scoring for fit, clarity, value, urgency, risk, and evidence

    • match.

  • Competitive analysis, product SWOT, partner matrices, and market-research

    • briefs that can feed product design, roadmap choices, and customer interviews.

  • Review-first wizards that open the source page, draft notes, evidence, and

    • follow-up checklist without sending anything automatically.

  • Inbox grooming that groups opportunities, rejections, follow-ups, and next

    • steps for operator review.

Where It Fits

This is not a black-box outreach bot. It is a practical product-discovery, sales, and marketing operations system for an operator who needs better visibility and cleaner follow-through.

The same architecture can support:

Area Example use
Product management Build competitive analyses, product SWOTs, partner matrices, market-research briefs, and customer interview guides.
Operations Find workflow automation, dispatch, reporting, and process-cleanup opportunities.
Development Track integration, scripting, data extraction, and internal-tool requests.
Data management Prioritize CSV cleanup, BI data prep, CRM cleanup, and reporting-data work.
Marketing automation Build lead lists, score lead quality, and organize follow-up evidence as one input to product and go-to-market decisions.

Market research brief Synthesize CX signals, public evidence, and market behavior into roadmap input.
Customer interview brief Prepare an interview.md style document with personas, hypotheses, questions, and evidence prompts.

Review-First Flow

  1. Collect public-safe source cards or import a CSV.
  2. Normalize fields and apply scoring rules.
  3. Build a recommendation list.
  4. Review recommended opportunities.
  5. Open a guided action wizard for the selected row.
  6. Draft notes, outreach, or proposal material separately.
  7. Track email responses and next steps in reviewed folders.

Every external action remains operator-controlled.

Publication Posture

The first publishable assets are Markdown briefs and synthetic scripts. Live scrapers, inbox readers, and platform-specific action wizards require separate privacy and terms-of-use review before any public code release.

Product Risk Analysis

How FRAME Reads Procurement Risk

FRAME treats procurement risk as a product discovery funnel, not just a technical integration checklist.

Sources
Public vendor documentation, supplier enablement terms, procurement learning material, cXML and PunchOut references, Sigma-Aldrich eProcurement content, and synthetic FRAME fixtures.
Research Approach
Each provider lane is mapped by access model, setup path, transaction lifecycle, likely owner, open question, and claim boundary. No private tenant access is implied.
Risk Reporting
Risks are reported as drop-off stages: access, supplier master, catalog discovery, PunchOut auth, cart return, approval, PO, invoice/ASN, support, and adoption.
Dashboard Integration
The risk map feeds partner matrices, SWOT, sprint backlog items, launch-health metrics, and BI-ready dashboard signals such as cart return exceptions and PO-to-invoice lag.
