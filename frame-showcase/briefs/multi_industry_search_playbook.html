Multi-Industry Search Playbook

FRAME discovery workflows are most useful when the same structure can be tuned for different markets.

The operator changes the source list and scoring lens, but keeps the core automation pattern:

  1. collect source cards
  2. normalize fields
  3. score fit and urgency
  4. generate a recommendation list
  5. open a reviewed action wizard
  6. track follow-up and outcomes

Industry Lenses

Lens Useful signals

Data management CSV, Excel, SQL, BI, CRM cleanup, data quality, reporting prep, dashboard input
Marketing automation lead list, campaign operations, CRM hygiene, outreach tracking, segmentation

Themed Source Sets

Each lens can use its own source set:

  • public RFP sites
  • freelance and contractor marketplaces
  • industry association pages
  • partner directories
  • CRM exports
  • saved search pages
  • reviewed email alerts

The point is not to scrape everything. The point is to make repeated research reviewable, explainable, and easier to resume.

Recommendation Output

A strong recommendation list should show:

  • why the opportunity was selected
  • what industry lens matched
  • what evidence or service offering supports it
  • what the risk is
  • what the next reviewed action should be

This gives the operator a short, useful working list instead of another unstructured pile of links.

Product Risk Analysis

How FRAME Reads Procurement Risk

FRAME treats procurement risk as a product discovery funnel, not just a technical integration checklist.

Sources
Public vendor documentation, supplier enablement terms, procurement learning material, cXML and PunchOut references, Sigma-Aldrich eProcurement content, and synthetic FRAME fixtures.
Research Approach
Each provider lane is mapped by access model, setup path, transaction lifecycle, likely owner, open question, and claim boundary. No private tenant access is implied.
Risk Reporting
Risks are reported as drop-off stages: access, supplier master, catalog discovery, PunchOut auth, cart return, approval, PO, invoice/ASN, support, and adoption.
Dashboard Integration
The risk map feeds partner matrices, SWOT, sprint backlog items, launch-health metrics, and BI-ready dashboard signals such as cart return exceptions and PO-to-invoice lag.
