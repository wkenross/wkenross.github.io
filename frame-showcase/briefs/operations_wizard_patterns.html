Operations Wizard Patterns

FRAME operations wizards turn schedule pages, calendar-style operational screens, inventory reports, and workbook outputs into reviewed exception lists and guided next steps.

The public story is operator-controlled workflow support, not unattended scraping or automatic updates.

Scheduler Pattern

The scheduler lane supports schedule-driven operations:

  • monitor schedule and calendar-style pages
  • normalize exported schedule, allocation, contract, and incident tables
  • identify schedule laggards, expiring contracts, allocation issues, and incomplete operational actions
  • prepare notification queues or reminder drafts for review
  • support CRM and legacy operations-system update queues

Inventory Wizard Pattern

The inventory lane supports inventory and supply reconciliation:

  • ask the operator which terminal, product, or report date is in scope
  • collect or confirm inventory-source data
  • collect or confirm operations-management source data
  • compare inventory, allocation, supply, truck, and related totals
  • show discrepancies and proposed updates
  • apply only approved workbook or report updates
  • write a run summary for audit and handoff

Guardrails

  • Do not publish private portal URLs.
  • Do not publish private workbook data, raw emails, screenshots, or customer records.
  • Do not hard-code browser profiles or account state.
  • Keep browser/site automation disabled unless a proxy or synthetic source is explicitly configured.
  • Present notification and CRM updates as reviewed queues, not automatic sends or unattended account changes.

Why It Fits FRAME

These workflows are another example of the FRAME pattern: gather source evidence, normalize it into tables, rank or flag exceptions, prepare the next action, and keep the operator visibly in control.
