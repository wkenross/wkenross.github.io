Price Digests Solution Brief

Price Digests is the B2B/B2C case study for vehicle valuation, insurance risk, insurer API delivery, public-sector valuation, and product analytics.

Product Story

The solution helped users reason about commercial vehicle value and related workflows:

  • commercial vehicle underwriters and insurers needed structured valuation,

    • specification, equipment, and risk context

  • insurer-facing systems needed API/data delivery so workflows did not depend

    • only on manual portal use

  • API delivery needed contract thinking: endpoint shape, response quality,

    • monitoring, issue triage, and clear communication with consuming systems

  • tax authorities and consumers needed defensible valuation workflows for

    • public-sector and consumer-facing decisions

  • product managers needed usage, adoption, support, and backlog evidence to

    • prioritize the next work

FRAME Connection

  • sr_b2b_b2c for the product narrative
  • sr_agile_pm for Jira-ready epics and stories

  • sr_tech_proj_mgmt for Pendo-style SaaS portal operations

API Testing And Adapter Relevance

Price Digests is useful for the B2B eCommerce Adapter story because it adds the API-product and endpoint-monitoring side of the FRAME experience. Procurement adapters need the same discipline:

  • define the expected request and response contract;
  • validate required fields and data quality;
  • monitor endpoint behavior over time;
  • connect failures to support, backlog, and roadmap decisions;
  • report health in a way product, engineering, CX, and commercial teams can

    • understand.

API Fortress, acquired by Sauce Labs in 2020, is a useful modern reference point for this kind of continuous API testing and monitoring. FRAME does not claim to replace Sauce Labs; it uses the same product idea at prototype scale: make service and adapter behavior testable before launch and visible after go-live.

Product Risk Analysis

How FRAME Reads Procurement Risk

FRAME treats procurement risk as a product discovery funnel, not just a technical integration checklist.

Sources
Public vendor documentation, supplier enablement terms, procurement learning material, cXML and PunchOut references, Sigma-Aldrich eProcurement content, and synthetic FRAME fixtures.
Research Approach
Each provider lane is mapped by access model, setup path, transaction lifecycle, likely owner, open question, and claim boundary. No private tenant access is implied.
Risk Reporting
Risks are reported as drop-off stages: access, supplier master, catalog discovery, PunchOut auth, cart return, approval, PO, invoice/ASN, support, and adoption.
Dashboard Integration
The risk map feeds partner matrices, SWOT, sprint backlog items, launch-health metrics, and BI-ready dashboard signals such as cart return exceptions and PO-to-invoice lag.

Publication rule: examples use public-safe or synthetic data only. They do not connect to live websites, mailboxes, CRM systems, marketplace accounts, or procurement tenants.
