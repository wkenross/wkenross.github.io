Product Discovery Research Kit

Created: 2026-06-30

Status: active B2B eCommerce Adapter discovery artifact.

Purpose

This kit repositions the resume, research, ForHire, recommendation, and procurement adapter tools as product-management discovery tools rather than only RFP response helpers.

The same FRAME workflow that can rank opportunities can also jump-start:

  • competitive analysis;
  • product SWOT;
  • partner matrices;
  • market and CX research;
  • customer interview guides;
  • product brief inputs;
  • roadmap and backlog candidates.

That maps directly to product-management requirements around product design, discovery with engineering and CX, market behavior, roadmap decisions, OKRs/metrics, and user stories with acceptance criteria.

Product Discovery Outputs

Output What FRAME Produces Product-Management Use
Competitive analysis Structured comparison of products, providers, capabilities, pricing clues, integration patterns, proof points, and gaps Identify differentiators, risks, roadmap themes, and customer-value arguments
Product SWOT Strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats grouped from source evidence Support ideation, prioritization, messaging, and mitigation planning
Partner matrix Side-by-side partner/vendor/platform comparison with capability, integration style, maturity, access need, risk, and prototype value Support architecture decisions, partner sequencing, and buy/build/wrap thinking
Competitive SWOT Competitor map, SWOT, headwinds, and differentiation levers Helps identify market differentiation and roadmap decisions
Market research brief Synthesis of public market behavior, CX signals, customer friction, procurement trends, and analytics candidates Convert external and customer evidence into roadmap and OKR inputs
Customer interview guide interview.md style artifact with personas, hypotheses, questions, evidence prompts, and follow-up fields Prepare product/customer discovery conversations with buyers, CX, engineering, procurement, and partners
Persona day-in-the-life First-value day, friction points, admin tasks, and dashboard needs by platform Helps the product manager understand customer segments and shape interview questions

Partner Matrix Extension

The procurement vendor matrix is now more than a technical comparison. Treat it as a product discovery tool:

Partner Dimension Question
Customer relevance Is the provider named by target customers or public ecosystem pages?
Integration pattern Is the likely path hosted catalog, PunchOut/cXML, OCI, EDI, API, supplier portal, or middleware?
Access model Can FRAME prototype from public docs, or does progress require buyer/platform-provider tenant access?
Business value Which customer friction or operating metric would improve if this lane works?
Risk What is closed, expensive, uncertain, or dependent on customer credentials?
Sprint value What fixture, parser, dashboard, or story would teach the most in two weeks?

Product SWOT Seed

Quadrant B2B Adapter Seed
Strengths FRAME can turn public vendor research into fixtures, validation, SQL/BI output, backlog items, and public-safe dashboard evidence quickly.
Weaknesses FRAME does not currently own production tenants for JAGGAER, SAP, Oracle, Coupa, Workday, or buyer-specific procurement environments.
Opportunities Supplier-side prototypes, launch-health dashboards, customer interview guides, partner matrices, and reviewed AI summaries can reduce early discovery ambiguity.
Threats Closed enterprise platforms, customer-specific security rules, credential access, and overclaiming production experience can weaken credibility if not handled carefully.

Customer Interview Guide Shape

Recommended file name for opportunity/customer discovery:


interview.md

Recommended sections:

  1. Customer segment and persona.
  2. Current procurement platform and buying flow.
  3. Catalog, PunchOut, hosted-file, PO, invoice, ASN, and exception pain.
  4. Data and dashboard needs.
  5. Security, credential, and tenant-access boundaries.
  6. Current support and CX signals.
  7. Success metrics and OKRs.
  8. Product differentiation opportunities.
  9. Risks and unanswered questions.
  10. Backlog candidates and acceptance criteria.

Product Risk Analysis

How FRAME Reads Procurement Risk

FRAME treats procurement risk as a product discovery funnel, not just a technical integration checklist.

Sources
Public vendor documentation, supplier enablement terms, procurement learning material, cXML and PunchOut references, Sigma-Aldrich eProcurement content, and synthetic FRAME fixtures.
Research Approach
Each provider lane is mapped by access model, setup path, transaction lifecycle, likely owner, open question, and claim boundary. No private tenant access is implied.
Risk Reporting
Risks are reported as drop-off stages: access, supplier master, catalog discovery, PunchOut auth, cart return, approval, PO, invoice/ASN, support, and adoption.
Dashboard Integration
The risk map feeds partner matrices, SWOT, sprint backlog items, launch-health metrics, and BI-ready dashboard signals such as cart return exceptions and PO-to-invoice lag.

Publication rule: examples use public-safe or synthetic data only. They do not connect to live websites, mailboxes, CRM systems, marketplace accounts, or procurement tenants.
