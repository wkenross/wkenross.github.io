Product Research Recommendation Workflow

The recommendation workflow turns many small product, market, partner, customer, and opportunity signals into a short, ranked list that an operator can actually review.

Input Sources

The public-safe pattern supports:

  • public RFP and procurement pages
  • competitor product pages and commerce portals
  • partner, vendor, and platform documentation
  • customer-segment research and CX notes that have been reviewed for privacy
  • freelancer and service-marketplace skill pages
  • industry association opportunity pages
  • partner directories and vendor lists
  • product-management, operations, development, and data-management communities
  • imported CSVs from CRM exports or lead research
  • email alerts that have already been reviewed for privacy

Opportunity Card

Each source becomes a structured card:

Field Purpose
source where the signal came from
organization buyer, company, agency, or marketplace client
title product, partner, opportunity, RFP, project, or lead title
industry product, operations, development, data, marketing, or other
url reviewed source link
scope short description of the product question, partner capability, or work
due date date, deadline, or follow-up window
value band rough value, budget signal, or priority tier
risk flags ambiguity, compliance, low budget, or poor fit
recommended action review, research, draft, follow up, or decline

The same card structure can support product-management outputs:

Output Extra Fields
Competitive analysis competitor, differentiator, comparable capability, proof link
Product SWOT quadrant, evidence, implication, action candidate
Partner matrix partner type, integration pattern, maturity, dependency, risk
Market research brief market signal, customer segment, CX implication, metric candidate
Customer interview brief persona, hypothesis, question, evidence prompt, follow-up owner

Scoring Model

The scoring model is intentionally explainable:

  • fit to services offered
  • clarity of scope
  • urgency or deadline value
  • budget or value signal
  • industry alignment
  • availability of evidence or case studies
  • customer impact
  • technical feasibility
  • partner or platform maturity
  • ability to inform roadmap, OKRs, or sprint backlog
  • risk and compliance concerns

The output is a recommendation list or product-discovery brief, not an automatic response queue.

Wizard Pattern

For a selected recommendation, the wizard can open:

  • the source page
  • the matching service-offering page
  • competitive analysis notes
  • SWOT notes
  • partner matrix row
  • market research brief
  • customer interview brief
  • a proposal or outreach draft
  • relevant case-study evidence
  • the related inbox thread
  • a checklist for reviewed next steps

The wizard should stop before any external submission, profile update, or outbound message.

Public-Safe Example

The staged script marketing_recommendation_builder.py reads synthetic opportunity rows and writes JSON, CSV, and Markdown recommendations. It demonstrates the scoring pattern without touching live sites or inboxes.

Product Risk Analysis

How FRAME Reads Procurement Risk

FRAME treats procurement risk as a product discovery funnel, not just a technical integration checklist.

Sources
Public vendor documentation, supplier enablement terms, procurement learning material, cXML and PunchOut references, Sigma-Aldrich eProcurement content, and synthetic FRAME fixtures.
Research Approach
Each provider lane is mapped by access model, setup path, transaction lifecycle, likely owner, open question, and claim boundary. No private tenant access is implied.
Risk Reporting
Risks are reported as drop-off stages: access, supplier master, catalog discovery, PunchOut auth, cart return, approval, PO, invoice/ASN, support, and adoption.
Dashboard Integration
The risk map feeds partner matrices, SWOT, sprint backlog items, launch-health metrics, and BI-ready dashboard signals such as cart return exceptions and PO-to-invoice lag.

Publication rule: examples use public-safe or synthetic data only. They do not connect to live websites, mailboxes, CRM systems, marketplace accounts, or procurement tenants.
