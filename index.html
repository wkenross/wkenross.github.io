FRAME approach

Review-first automation with service boundaries.

FRAME is a practical automation framework for bridging legacy operations and closed tools toward service-oriented, BI-ready, and AI-ready work.

It does not pretend every enterprise platform can or should be locally installed. Instead, it documents what the business depends on, preserves the reports and checkpoints people already trust, and builds wrapper scripts, fixtures, data handoffs, and reviewed actions around realistic access points.

What FRAME Produces

  • Service-boundary notes and endpoint candidates.
  • SQL, BI, CRM, and report handoff artifacts.
  • PowerShell orchestration and Python operation scripts.
  • Agile-ready epics, stories, acceptance criteria, and release notes.
  • Public-safe HTML pages that explain the work without exposing private systems.

Review-First Rule

Automation prepares the evidence, drafts, browser sessions, reports, and next actions. A person stays in control of submissions, sends, publication, and business decisions.
